Alash Orda

Alash Orda party

Party "Alash" (kaz. "Alash" party) is a socio-political national liberation movement that united the Kazakh and Kyrgyz intelligentsia, created in 1917 by journalist and publicist Alikhan Bukeikhanov, linguist, poet and translator Akhmet Baitursynov and Dulatov, ideologically affiliated with the Cadet Party. The party chairman is Alikhan Bukeikhanov. The official organ of the party is the Kazakh newspaper.

History of Alash

It was formed from a group of members of the Cadet Party - ethnic Kazakhs, which took shape during the events of 1905. At the First All-Kazakh Congress (at that time the All-Kirgiz Congress), held in Orenburg from July 21 to 28, 1917, the Alash party was organized. At the same congress, decisions were made on 14 issues, including such key ones as the form of state structure of Russia (parliamentary federal republic), the republic of Kazakh regions, land management of the Kazakh people, attitude to religion, the question of the status of Kazakh women, preparation of elections to the Constituent meeting. The draft program of the "Alash" party, published before the elections to the Constituent Assembly of Russia, set as priorities universal suffrage, proportional national representation, a democratic Russian federal republic with a president and a legislative Duma, equality of the republics that are part of Russia, democratic freedoms, secession churches from the state, equality of languages, etc. In November 1917, in the elections to the Constituent Assembly, the Alash party received the majority of votes and 43 seats. By the number of votes received in the elections to the Constituent Assembly (262,404 votes), "Alash" took 8th place among fifty parties that existed in Russia on the eve of the October Revolution At the Second All-Kazakh Congress in December 1917, the Alash Republic was proclaimed and a temporary People's Council was formed, which was given the name "Alash-Orda". Provided for the subsequent approval of the Constitution of the Alash Republic by the All-Russian Constituent Assembly.

Liquidation by USSR

It was liquidated by the Bolsheviks and ceased to exist in 1920. The Bolsheviks who came to power, the Alash party was immediately abolished, and all its leaders, despite the amnesty, were shot in the 30s.
